# Phasmophobia Ghost Identification: Complete Reference Guide

This corrected edition updates the earlier report against the official Phasmophobia wiki and adjusts several mechanics where community shorthand was less precise than the wiki's current wording or numbers.[cite:1][cite:3]

## Evidence system

Phasmophobia currently uses seven evidence types: EMF Level 5, D.O.T.S. Projector, Ultraviolet, Freezing Temperatures, Ghost Orb, Ghost Writing, and Spirit Box.[cite:1] Each ghost has three evidences on standard settings, while Nightmare hides one and Insanity leaves only one visible evidence.[cite:1]

Ultraviolet evidence lasts **2 minutes by default** and **1 minute on Insanity**, which matches the official wiki and did not need correction.[cite:1] Ghost Orbs remain a camera-only evidence, while The Mimic uniquely produces a fourth, fake Ghost Orb evidence in addition to its normal three-evidence set.[cite:1][cite:3]

## High-impact corrections

The largest correction is that **Banshee can change favorite rooms** as of patch 0.13.0, so it should no longer be treated as a ghost that permanently anchors to one room.[cite:3] Goryo still remains the key ghost that never changes favorite room under normal play, aside from Monkey Paw interference.[cite:3]

The Demon crucifix bonus must also be expressed in **tier-specific ranges**, not as a flat "5m vs 3m" simplification.[cite:3] The official wiki gives Demon crucifix ranges as **4.5m / 6m / 7.5m** for Tier I / II / III, compared with normal ranges of **3m / 4m / 5m**.[cite:3]

Hantu's freezing breath during hunts also needed tightening: when the breaker is off, the Hantu can show freezing breath **in any room**, not only in already-cold rooms.[cite:3] Mare likewise needed nuance: it cannot turn on normal wall light switches, but it **can** turn on TVs, computers, the fuse box, and motion-activated lights.[cite:3]

Phantom and Wraith also needed more precise wording.[cite:3] A Phantom's special roam is a **walk** to the player, which means it can still trigger motion sensors and disturb salt, while Wraith uses teleport-style movement and can leave EMF at the destination with a 33% chance of EMF 5.[cite:3]

## Updated ghost corrections

### Banshee

Banshee still targets one player at a time in multiplayer and still has the distinctive Parabolic Microphone shriek, but it should **not** be described as permanently fixed to one favorite room anymore.[cite:3] The official wiki says it can now change favorite rooms, even though it also has a strong tendency to roam toward its chosen target.[cite:3]

### Demon

The Demon still has the shortest standard hunt cooldown and can hunt at any sanity with its ability, but its crucifix advantage must be described by tier.[cite:3] The correct official values are 4.5m, 6m, and 7.5m for Tier I, II, and III crucifixes respectively.[cite:3]

### Hantu

The Hantu speed endpoints used in the earlier report were fine, but the official wiki provides the full staircase of temperature-based speeds rather than only the warmest and coldest values.[cite:3] Its freezing breath tell is tied to the breaker being off, not to the room itself already being cold.[cite:3]

### Mare

Mare still never turns on standard light switches and still hunts earlier in darkness than in lit rooms.[cite:3] The corrected wording is that Mare may still activate certain powered objects such as TVs, computers, the fuse box, and motion-sensor lights, so "turning something on" is too broad a rule-out phrase.[cite:3]

### Onryo

The main Onryo correction is timing precision.[cite:3] After the third candle blowout, the forced hunt can be delayed by **up to 6 seconds**, not 10 seconds, and the wiki is much more careful than community shorthand about when this trigger is suppressed or postponed by hunt states, cooldowns, or Incense effects.[cite:3]

### Phantom

Phantom still disappears when photographed during an event, but its sanity effect is better described as **0.5% per second within 10m in pseudo-line-of-sight** during events and hunts, rather than a vague statement that it drains sanity "twice as fast."[cite:3] Its special approach to a player is also a physical walk, which means footprints, salt disturbance, and motion sensor triggers remain possible.[cite:3]

### Shade

Shade is still one of the shyest ghosts and still cannot hunt from the same room as a player, but the earlier wording about interactions was too absolute.[cite:3] The official wiki makes clear that Shade can still interact into a player's room from an adjacent room because interaction range can cross room boundaries.[cite:3]

### Thaye

Thaye does age over time near players, but the earlier report simplified the timing too much.[cite:3] The official wiki says the first age attempt happens **1 minute after the exit door is first opened**; after that, the retry cadence depends on whether players are nearby, with 1 to 2 minute intervals when someone is within 3m and 30-second retries when no player is nearby.[cite:3]

### The Twins

The Twins can still produce near-simultaneous interactions in two ranges, but the wiki frames this as a specific ability occurrence rather than a general flavor rule.[cite:3] The dual interaction is an ability trigger that occurs **20% of the time**, with the remaining 80% using normal single-range interaction behavior.[cite:3]

### Wraith

The salt rule remains one of the strongest Wraith tells: it never disturbs salt.[cite:3] The correction is that its teleport-related EMF occurs at the **destination near the player**, and the EMF result has a **33% chance to be EMF 5** instead of ordinary EMF 2.[cite:3]

### Yurei

Yurei still has the distinctive full-door movement ability tied to a sanity hit, but the wiki sharpens two details.[cite:3] The ability requires an open door in the current room, and the special close happens without a creaking sound, which is a stronger recognition cue than the earlier wording captured.[cite:3]

## Still-correct points retained from the earlier report

Several important numbers in the original guide were already correct against the official wiki, including UV duration, Deogen's close/far hunt speed behavior, Goryo's camera-only D.O.T.S. requirement, Jinn's breaker dependence, Spirit's 180-second Incense test, and Yokai's 2.5m voice/electronics sensitivity.[cite:1][cite:3] The newer ghosts such as Dayan, Gallu, and Obambo were based partly on recent community and wiki material, but the corrections requested here mainly affected older ghosts whose community shorthand tends to drift away from exact wiki wording over time.[cite:19][cite:30][cite:38]

## Practical identification notes

For practical play, the best adjustment is to treat the official wiki as the source of truth for **numeric values**, while still using community content for **fieldcraft**, hunt-reading, and efficient test setups.[cite:3] Community guides remain extremely useful for ideas like loop tests, candle setups, and audio recognition, but the exact meters, seconds, thresholds, and exception cases should be checked against the official wiki whenever precision matters.[cite:3]
